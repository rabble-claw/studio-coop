# Studio Software Pricing Examples (Web Scan, March 6, 2026)

This is a quick benchmark set you can use when you do not have direct invoices from studios yet.

- https://www.mindbodyonline.com/business/pricing
- https://www.mindbodyonline.com/company/legal/terms-of-service/supplemental-terms-for-the-marketing-services

2. Vagaro: US base price shown as `$23.99/month` with one calendar included; each additional employee calendar is `$10/month` (up to seven licenses). Australia support pricing shows `$45/month` base and `$15/month` per additional calendar.
- https://support.vagaro.com/hc/en-us/articles/22781768988187-Vagaro-Plans-Pricing-and-Premium-Features
- https://support.vagaro.com/hc/en-us/articles/39346663108763-Manage-Your-Vagaro-Subscription
- https://www.vagaro.com/pro/pricing

3. WellnessLiving: Starter `$69/mo`, Business `$199/mo`, BusinessPro `$349/mo` (with promotional discounted months shown on page).
- https://www.wellnessliving.com/pricing/

4. Studio Pro (DanceStudio-Pro): `$45`, `$75`, `$150` monthly plan tiers.
- https://info.dancestudio-pro.com/pricing-google

5. Dance Studio Manager: flat `$40/month` positioning on pricing page.
- https://www.dancestudiomanager.com/pricing/

6. Jackrabbit Dance: Jackrabbit Plus starts at `$93/month` plus `$169` one-time setup fee; enterprise option shown at `$245/month`.
- https://www.jackrabbitdance.com/pricing/

- https://momence.com/pricing

## 3) Practical Benchmark Bands for Studio-Coop Modeling

Until we have direct interview invoices, use these bands:

1. Budget/lean tools: `NZD 70-180/month`
2. Mid-tier all-in-one: `NZD 180-450/month`
3. Premium/opaque enterprise-style setups: `NZD 450-1300+/month`

Suggested default for early coop comparisons: `NZD 220/month` software baseline per studio before payment processing.

## 4) Provisional Segment Benchmarks (NZD/Month)

Use these only until owner interviews provide real invoices:

1. Pole/Aerial studios: `NZD 180`
2. Dance studios: `NZD 200`
3. Yoga studios: `NZD 240`
4. Mixed fitness/BJJ/gym studios: `NZD 280`

Reasoning:

1. Dance-focused tools publish lower starting prices than large all-in-one suites.
2. Yoga/wellness stacks are often on higher all-in-one plans.
3. Mixed studios can require broader scheduling + staff + add-on complexity.

## 5) Notes for Discovery Calls

Ask each owner for:

1. Base subscription fee.
2. Per-staff or per-calendar fee.
3. Add-ons (marketing, forms, app, payroll, integrations).
4. Setup/onboarding fees.
5. Any marketplace/discovery/lead fees.
6. Contract length and exit/migration fees.

That will let us replace benchmark assumptions with true cost-of-ownership by segment (pole, yoga, dance, mixed).

## 6) Regional Expansion Inputs (Add to Interview Script)

For regions like LATAM, collect this before launch decisions:

1. Which payment methods are mandatory locally (cards, bank transfer, cash voucher, local wallets).
2. Payment-failure rate by method and country.
3. Payout speed expectations to studios.
4. Tax/invoicing requirements for SaaS and payment flows.
5. Local support language expectations and support-hour windows.

Without these inputs, benchmark pricing alone is not enough to model viable adoption.

# Studio Co-operative Limited
## Draft Rules of a Multi-Stakeholder Co-operative Society

*Registered under the Co-operative and Community Benefit Societies Act 2014*

---

### Part 1: Name and Registered Office

1. The name of the Society shall be **Studio Co-operative Limited**.
2. The registered office of the Society shall be in England.

### Part 2: Objects

3. The objects of the Society shall be to carry on business for the benefit of its Members and the wider cooperative community by:
   - (a) Developing, operating, and maintaining a cooperative platform for fitness and wellness studio management;
   - (b) Providing scheduling, payments, community, and business management tools to fitness and wellness studios;
   - (c) Operating as a genuine cooperative in accordance with the internationally recognised cooperative values and principles as defined by the International Cooperative Alliance;
   - (d) Enabling Member studios to collectively own and govern the platform they depend on;
   - (e) Promoting cooperative principles within the fitness and wellness industry.

### Part 3: Powers

4. The Society shall have the power to do anything which is calculated to further its objects or is conducive or incidental to doing so, including but not limited to:
   - (a) Develop, acquire, and license software and technology;
   - (b) Enter into contracts, agreements, and arrangements;
   - (c) Employ staff and engage contractors;
   - (d) Borrow money and grant security;
   - (e) Invest surplus funds;
   - (f) Purchase, lease, or otherwise acquire property;
   - (g) Affiliate or cooperate with other cooperative organisations;
   - (h) Do all such other things as are incidental or conducive to the above objects.

### Part 4: Membership

5. **Stakeholder classes.** The Society shall have the following classes of Member:
   - (a) **Studio Members** — Fitness and wellness studios that subscribe to and use the platform;
   - (b) **Worker Members** — Individuals engaged in the day-to-day operations, development, and maintenance of the Society's platform and services;
   - (c) **Supporter Members** — Individuals or organisations that support the objects of the Society but do not fall within the above categories.

6. **Admission.** An application for membership shall be made in writing (which may include electronic form) and shall be approved by the Board. The Board shall not unreasonably refuse any application.

7. **Minimum membership.** The Society shall have no fewer than three Members.

8. **Member commitment.** Each Studio Member shall pay a periodic subscription as determined by the Board from time to time. The initial subscription shall be AUD $99 per month (or equivalent in local currency).

9. **Withdrawal.** A Member may withdraw from the Society by giving not less than one month's notice in writing to the Secretary.

10. **Expulsion.** A Member may be expelled by a resolution carried by not less than two-thirds of those present and voting at a General Meeting, provided that:
    - (a) The Member has been given at least 28 days' notice of the meeting and the resolution;
    - (b) The Member has been given a reasonable opportunity to be heard.

### Part 5: General Meetings

11. **Annual General Meeting.** The Society shall hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) within six months of the end of each financial year.

12. **Special General Meetings.** A Special General Meeting may be called by:
    - (a) A resolution of the Board; or
    - (b) A requisition signed by not less than 10% of Members or 3 Members (whichever is greater).

13. **Notice.** Not less than 14 days' notice of any General Meeting shall be given to all Members. Notice may be given by electronic means.

14. **Quorum.** The quorum for a General Meeting shall be 3 Members or 20% of the total membership (whichever is greater), present in person or by electronic means.

15. **Voting.** Each Member shall have one vote regardless of stakeholder class. Decisions shall be made by simple majority unless otherwise specified in these Rules. In the event of an equality of votes, the Chair shall have a casting vote.

16. **Remote participation.** Members may participate in and vote at General Meetings by electronic means approved by the Board.

### Part 6: Board of Directors

17. **Composition.** The Board shall consist of not fewer than 3 and not more than 12 Directors, drawn from the membership. The Board shall endeavour to include representation from each stakeholder class.

18. **Election.** Directors shall be elected by the Members at the AGM. Each Director shall serve a term of 3 years and shall be eligible for re-election.

19. **Officers.** The Board shall elect from among its number a Chair, a Secretary, and a Treasurer.

20. **Board meetings.** The Board shall meet at least quarterly. The quorum for Board meetings shall be 3 Directors or one-third of the Directors (whichever is greater).

21. **Board powers.** The Board shall manage the affairs of the Society and may exercise all powers of the Society not reserved to a General Meeting by these Rules or by law.

### Part 7: Finance

22. **Financial year.** The financial year of the Society shall end on 31 March.

23. **Application of profits.** The profits of the Society in any year shall be applied as follows:
    - (a) First, to a general reserve as the Board may determine;
    - (b) Second, to the payment of a dividend or patronage refund to Studio Members in proportion to their use of the Society's services;
    - (c) Third, to any social, educational, or cooperative purpose as the General Meeting may determine.

24. **Share capital.** The Society shall not issue shares to Members. Membership shall be acquired by application and payment of any subscription due.

25. **Borrowing.** The Board may borrow money for the purposes of the Society, subject to any limits set by a General Meeting.

### Part 8: Administration

26. **Audit.** The accounts of the Society shall be audited or examined in accordance with the requirements of the Co-operative and Community Benefit Societies Act 2014.

27. **Registered address.** The Society shall maintain a registered office in England or Wales.

28. **Amendments.** These Rules may be amended by a resolution carried by not less than two-thirds of those present and voting at a General Meeting, subject to FCA approval where required.

29. **Dissolution.** The Society may be dissolved by an instrument of dissolution or by the winding up of the Society in accordance with the Act. On dissolution, after satisfying all debts and liabilities, any remaining assets shall not be distributed among Members but shall be transferred to one or more cooperative organisations as a General Meeting may determine.

### Part 9: Dispute Resolution

30. **Internal disputes.** Any dispute between a Member and the Society shall first be referred to the Board for resolution. If the dispute cannot be resolved, it may be referred to mediation by a mediator agreed between the parties or, failing agreement, appointed by Co-operatives UK.

### Part 10: Interpretation

31. In these Rules:
    - "the Act" means the Co-operative and Community Benefit Societies Act 2014;
    - "the Board" means the Board of Directors of the Society;
    - "Director" means a member of the Board;
    - "Member" means a member of the Society;
    - "the Society" means Studio Co-operative Limited.
